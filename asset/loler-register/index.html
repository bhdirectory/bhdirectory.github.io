Arrangements for Safe Lifting Operations and Lifting Equipment (LOLER)

Whilst we try to avoid the need for staff to undertake manual handling tasks, there is an unavoidable need for assisting residents who need help with mobility. The company has conducted a risk assessment with regard to manual handling operations undertaken by staff on residents and only trained and competant staff are authroised to carry out the task. To assist staff in these manual handling tasks, the company provides a wide range of mechanical aids. There are 19 hoists and 3 lifting bath chairs which come under LOLER regulations. Of the 19 hoists, 9 are mobile and 10 are fixed overhead ceiling hoists. Of the 9 mobile hoists, 2 are standing aids. All hoists are maintained over and above the recommended minimum standard. An annual service (which also includes a LOLER test) is supplemented by two further LOLER examinations visits from an independent engineer arranged as part of a comprehensive Engineering Insurance Policy which covers all patient lifting equipment. In addition to the 'pre-start' checks that all staff are asked to complete before each lifting operation, a monthly check ensures that the hoists are maintained in a safe and effective manner.
