All residents have access to a nursecall system intended for making efficient requests for services from Nursing and Care Staff as well as for summoning help in the event of an emergency. The standard call bells provided include a < a href="http://www.possum.co.uk/products/atom-2/">neck pendant (which can alternatively been worn around the wrist) and a < a href="http://www.possum.co.uk/products/wall-family/">static unit affixed to either bedroom wall (with a cabled press button for flexible positioning to suit the resident) or to the ensuite wall (with a pull cord). Additional devices include sensor mats, PIR units and other monitors which will be assessed and deployed according to need. The supplier also has a range of environmental control solutions which could be used in specific cases. All calls are transmitted to individual 'pager' units that care staff carry throughout their shift. In addition, a centrally located display shows current active bells. The nursecall system has a logger which produces reports every 24 hours. These reports are audited once a month to better understand how the system is being used. The logs provide a means of investigation should a concern about the responsiveness of the bell system be raised.
