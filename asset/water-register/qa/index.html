Risks identified by our water safety risk assessment are controlled by following the guidance in the HSE's Health Service Information Sheet No. 6 (HSIS6): Managing the risks from hot water and surfaces in health and social care and in HSG274: Legionnaires’ disease, Part 2: The control of legionella bacteria in hot and cold water systems.

Periodic Inspection

A third-party specialist inspect the water services every two years as part of a review of the risk assessment. Recommendations are acted upon as practicable.

Residential Water Temperature Check (TMV Function Test)

This monthly check ensures that outlets that may be accessible to our residents do not discharge water hotter than 44°C and, at the same time, that the hot water temperature is at least 50°C at the point it enters the thermostatic mixing valve. Any non-compliant tests that cannot be rectified at the time of the test are reported via the Maintenance Requests procedure and prioritised accordingly. This check ensures that all TMVs are checked for correct function every month as well as going a long way in fulfilling the need to check all outlets once a year.

TMV Servicing

Currently, TMVs are serviced in an ad hoc manner as and when issues are reported. We have installed a large number of new TMV3-rated taps in residents rooms in recent years rather than servicing the older TMV2 concealed units. The intention would be that the TMVs are serviced annually in accordance with the manufacturer’s instructions.

Sentinel Outlet Check

this test ensures that temperatures at sentinel taps (typically those nearest to and furthest from the cold tank, but may also include other key locations on long branches to zones or floor levels) is at the correct temperature. These outlets should be below 20°C within two minutes of running the cold tap and above 55°C (in line with healthcare premises) after a maximum of one minute on the hot water supply.
