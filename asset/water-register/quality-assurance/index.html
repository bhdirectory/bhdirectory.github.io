TMV Checks (Monthly)

Risks identified by our water safety risk assessment are controlled by following the guidance in the HSE's Health Service Information Sheet No. 6 (HSIS6): Managing the risks from hot water and surfaces in health and social care and in HSG274: Legionnaires’ disease, Part 2: The control of legionella bacteria in hot and cold water systems.

The monthly check ensures that outlets that may be accessible to our residents do not discharge water hotter than 44°C and, at the same time, that the hot water temperature is at least 50°C at the point it enters the thermostatic mixing valve.

Any non-compliant tests that cannot be rectified at the time of the test are reported via the Maintenance Requests procedure and prioritised accordingly.

Sentinel Outlet Checks (Monthly)

Check temperatures at sentinel taps (typically those nearest to and furthest from the cold tank, but may also include other key locations on long branches to zones or floor levels). These outlets should be below 20 °C within two minutes of running the cold tap. To identify any local heat gain, which might not be apparent after one minute, observe the thermometer reading during flushing
