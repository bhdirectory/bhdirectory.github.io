Data Protection Policy

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) which came into force in May 2018, strengthened your rights over any personal data that the Company holds on you. This Data Protection Policy is the overarching policy for data security and protection for Birtley House Group (hereafter referred to as "us", "we", or "our").

This policy is underpinned by the following:

  • Data Security Policy – details the management of records from creation to disposal. The policy outlines the measures that are taken to safeguard personal information and includes procedures for the ensuring compliance with payment card security standards and the reporting of any data security breach;
  • Network Security Policy – outlines procedures for securing our network and associated IT equipment including procedures in the event of a security failure or disaster affecting digital systems or mass loss of hardcopy information necessary to the day to day running of our organisation;
  • A Confidentiality Code of Conduct - which provides Staff with clear guidance on the disclosure of personal information.

Aims

The purpose of the Data Protection Policy is to support the 7 Caldicott Principles, the 10 Data Security Standards published by the National Data Guardian for Health and Care, the General Data Protection Regulation (2016), the forthcoming Data Protection Act (2018), the common law duty of confidentiality and all other relevant national legislation. We recognise data protection as a fundamental right and embrace the principle of Data Protection by Design and by Default.

Scope

As defined in the GDPR (2016), the Data Controller is:

the natural or legal person, public authority, agency or other body which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the processing of personal data; where the purposes and means of such processing are determined by Union or Member State law, the controller or the specific criteria for its nomination may be provided for by Union or Member State law.

The Company is both a Data Controller and a Data Processor and is appropriately registered with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), the UK authority for data protection.

We process the personal information of our staff and clients including special categories of data such as healthcare records and criminal records. This policy includes in its scope all data which we process either in hardcopy or electronic format. We also process CCTV images.

This policy applies to all staff, including temporary staff, our service users and third-party service providers.

Principles

We will be open and transparent with residents and those who lawfully act on their behalf in relation to their care and treatment. We will adhere to our Duty of Candour responsibilities as outlined in the Health and Social Care Act 2012.

We will establish and maintain policies to ensure compliance and maintain policies for the controlled and appropriate sharing of Resident and Staff information within the organisation and with other agencies, taking account all relevant legislation and consent.

We will undertake annual audits of our compliance with legal requirements.

We acknowledge our accountability in ensuring that personal data shall be:

  • processed lawfully, fairly and in a transparent manner (see our Privacy Notice);
  • collected for specified, explicit and legitimate purposes and not further processed in a manner that is incompatible with those purposes (see Our Lawful Bases for Processing Data);
  • adequate, relevant and limited to what is necessary in relation to the purposes for which they are processed and is kept in a form which permits identification of data subjects for no longer than is necessary for the purposes for which the personal data are processed (see the principle of Data Protection by Design and by Default);
  • accurate and kept up to date (see our Data Quality Principles);
  • processed in a manner that ensures appropriate security of the personal data (see our Data Security Policy).

We uphold the personal data rights outlined in the GDPR and as set out in our Your Data Rights:

  • The Right to be Informed;
  • The Right of Access;
  • The Right to Rectification;
  • The Right to Erasure;
  • The Right to Restrict Processing;
  • The Right to Data Portability;
  • The Right to Object;

Data Protection by Design and by Default

We shall implement appropriate organisational and technical measures to uphold the principles outlined above. We will integrate necessary safeguards to any data processing to meet regulatory requirements and to protect individual’s data rights. This implementation will consider the nature, scope, purpose and context of any processing and the risks to the rights and freedoms of individuals caused by the processing.

We shall uphold the principles of Data Protection by Design and by Default from the beginning of any data processing and during the planning and implementation of any new data process.

Any new high-risk data processing activities will be assessed using a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) before the processing commences.

All existing data processing has been recorded on our Information Asset Register (IAR). Each process has been risk assessed and is reviewed annually.

We ensure that, by default, personal data is only processed when necessary for specific purposes and that individuals are therefore protected against privacy risks.

In all processing of personal data, we use the least amount of identifiable data necessary to complete the work it is required for and we only keep the information for as long as it is required for the purposes of processing or any other legal requirement to retain it.

Data Quality Principles

We commit to ensuring that we comply with the Health and Social Care Act 2008 (Regulated Activities) Regulations 2014: Regulation 17(c) that we will

...maintain securely an accurate, complete and contemporaneous record in respect of each service user, including a record of the care and treatment provided to the service user and of decisions taken in relation to the care and treatment provided.

The Company acknowledges that the availability of accurate and timely data is vital for the safety of our residents and Staff and the safe and responsible running of our organisation.

We ensure accuracy in our data in both hardcopy and electronic records by making sure all data has the following characteristics:

  • Authentic – the data is what is claims to be, has been created or sent by the person who said that they created or sent it, and that this was done at the time claimed.
  • Reliable – the data is complete, accurate, has been created close to the time of the activity it records, and has been created by individuals with direct knowledge of the event it records.
  • Integrity – the data is complete and unaltered, it is also protected from being changed or altered by unauthorised persons, any alterations are clearly marked and the person who made them can be identified.
  • Useable – the data can be located when it is required for use and its context is clear in a contemporaneous record.

The principal purpose of our residents records is to record and communicate information about the individual and their care. The principal purpose of staff records is to record employment details for payroll and business planning purposes. To fulfil these purposes, we:

  • Use standardised structures and layouts for the contents of records as far as possible;
  • Ensure documentation reflects the continuum of care, that all care is person centred and that care records are viewable in chronological order;
  • Provide a clearly written care plan when care is being delivered by several members of the team, and we ensure that records are maintained and updated, and shared with everyone involved;
  • Train staff on the creation and use of records and provide training on good record keeping;
  • Have implemented a procedure that enables residents and staff to have access to their records as outlined in the Subject Access Request procedure.

The Registered Manager is responsible for the quality of records and making sure that staff understand their commitments in ensuring data quality;

Every member of staff who records information - whether hardcopy or electronic - has a contractual responsibility to ensure that the data is accurate and as complete as possible. This responsibility extends to any system the staff member has access to.

Our Lawful Bases for Processing Data

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) establishes 6 legal bases on which we can process your data: Consent, Contract, Legal Obligation, Vital Interests, Public Task and Legitimate Interest. For further information about these legal bases and fuller definitions, please refer to the ICO website

The Company will keep a record of the basis on which we are relying for each processing purpose as part of our Information Asset Register (IAR) and include details about the lawful bases under which we process data via our Privacy Notice and other publically available documents.

The Company has identified how the following Article 6 conditions for lawful processing are relevant to our activities:

6(1)(a) Consent

Where consent is deemed the most appropriate basis for processing data, the Company will ensure that informed and explicit consent will be obtained and documented in clear, accessible language and in an appropriate format. The individual can withdraw this consent at any time through processes which will have been explained to them. We will ensure that it is as easy to withdraw as to give consent.

6(1)(b) Contract

In general, our lawful basis for the majority of our processing of personal information is in order to fulfil our contractual obligations to both staff and service users. Processing under this basis must be necessary to fulfill a contractual obligation. Where this data is considered Special Category Data, a separate condition will also be required (see below: Special Category Data).

6(1)(c) Legal Obligation

Where the Company is obliged to process personal data in order to comply with the law and the process is reasonable and proportionate in achieving compliance. Where there is a Legal Obligation, the law in question will be cited in the Information Asset Register. Under this basis, the data subject will have no Right to Erasure, Right to Data Portability, or Right to Object to the processing.

6(1)(e) Public Task

In exceptional circumstances, the Company may be compelled to act to protect an individual at risk of harm or if we believe an individual is a risk to themselves or others. In such cases, we may share personal information we hold with the police or other agencies under this basis. Under this basis, the data subject will have no Right to Erasure or Right to Data Portability, but would have a Right to Object to the processing.

6(1)(f) Legitimate Interest

In general, the Company will only process personal data under a legitimate interest basis if this is in a manner that the data subject would expect and where there is only a minimal privacy impact. In these instances, the Company would always consider whether it can offer a means of opting out.

Special Category Data

Special Category Data is personal information that the GDPR considers more sensitive and requires further protection. In order to lawfully process Special Category Data, the Company will identify both a lawful basis under Article 6 and a separate condition for processing Special Category Data under Article 9.

The condition for processing Special Category Data which most commonly applies to us is Article 9(2)(h):

...the provision of health or social care or treatment...

Criminal Offence Data

Just as with Special Category Data, the Company must have a lawful basis under Article 6 to process Criminal Offence Data, however there are separate safeguards for protecting personal information relating to criminal convictions or offences which require compliance with Article 10. The Company has legal authority to carry out DBS checks on all staff and others likely to have direct contact with our service users. The Company will not keep a comprehensive register of criminal convictions.

Further details and examples of the types of information we process are available in our Privacy Notice

Our Standard Procedures for Handling Rights Requests

The IG Lead will maintain a record of any requests you make in regards to your rights and the outcome of such requests

We will respond to all requests within a 28 day period unless the request is particularly complex in which case we will let you know about any extension within the 28 day period and explain our reasons.

We would not usually charge a fee to comply with a request, however, if any request is manifestly unfounded or excessive we reserve the right to charge a reasonable fee or to refuse to uphold your request.

If we refuse to uphold a request we will explain why in writing and you will be informed of your legal rights to complain to the Information Commissioners Office (https://ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint/) and to seek a judicial remedy.

If applicable, where we may have appropriately shared that individual’s records with a third-party we will inform this third-party of the request if appropriate.

Confidential records of the deceased have the rights afford to them by the Duty of Confidentiality. Should any person wish to request access for any records of the deceased they should contact the IG Lead.

Your Right of Access (Subject Accesss Request)

In most cases, you have the right to obtain a copy of the personal data we hold about you as well as other supplementary information in order that you understand how and why we are using your personal data and to check we are doing so lawfully.

In order to make a Subject Access Request, the citizen or their legal representative, should contact the IG Lead. In all cases we will respond to a Subject Access Request without delay and in a timeframe not exceeding one month. Should the request be complex this may be extended to two months, however, we will inform the individual in writing of the extension and the reasons why it is required within one month. In order to process a Subject Access Request we will use reasonable means to verify the identity of the individual making the request. We will provide a copy of any information which it is lawful to provide free of charge. If further copies are required, we will charge a fee which will exclusively cover the administration costs of making copies. We will provide copies of the information requested in an appropriate format.

Any requests made in this regard will be treated in line with our Standard Procedures for Handling Requests.

Further details of how to make a Subject Access Request can be found in our Subject Access Requests: A Guide

Your Right to Rectification (Correction of Errors Procedures)

The GDPR includes a right for individuals to have inaccurate personal data rectified, or completed if it is incomplete. In order to request for their records to be rectified you should contact our IG Lead with the request for rectification. If the rectification is due the record being incomplete, then the individual should also provide the supplementary information to update the record.

Any requests made in this regard will be treated in line with our Standard Procedures for Handling Requests.

Your Right to Erasure ('Right to be Forgotten')

In certain circumstances, you may be entitled to have your personal data erased from our systems. This is also known as 'the right to be forgotten'.

You can request for your data to be erased in the following instances:

  • where the personal data is no longer necessary in relation to the purpose for which it was originally collected/processed;
  • when the individual withdraws consent;
  • when the individual objects to the processing and there is no overriding legitimate interest for continuing the processing;
  • the personal data was unlawfully processed;
  • the personal data must be erased in order to comply with a legal obligation;
  • the personal data is processed in relation to the offer of information society services to a child.

We will not be able to honour any requests to have personal data erased when the data is being processed for the following reasons:

  • to assess the working capacity of an employee;
  • to provide a medical diagnosis;
  • to provide health or social care or treatment or the management of health or social care systems and services;
  • to exercise the right of freedom of expression and information;
  • to comply with a legal obligation for the performance of a public interest task or exercise of official authority;
  • for public health purposes in the public interest;
  • archiving purposes in the public interest, scientific research historical research or statistical purposes;
  • the exercise or defence of legal claims.

Any requests made in this regard will be treated in line with our Standard Procedures for Handling Requests.

Right to Restrict Processing

In certain circumstances, you may be able to request that the personal data we are processing be restricted. In these circumstances, we may hold your data, but not use it.

You have the right to request we restrict the processing of your personal data in the following circumstances:

  • you contest the accuracy of the personal data we hold on you and you are verifying the accuracy of the data;
  • your data has been unlawfully processed (ie in breach of the lawfulness requirement of the first principle of the GDPR) and you oppose erasure and request restriction instead;
  • the Company no longer needs your personal data but you need us to keep it in order to establish, exercise or defend a legal claim;
  • you have objected to us processing your data and we are considering whether our legitimate grounds override yours.

Any requests made in this regard will be treated in line with our Standard Procedures for Handling Requests.

Right to Data Portability

The Right to Data Portability is intended to allow individuals to obtain and reuse their personal data for their own purposes across different services. However, due to the fact that we do not process personal information by automated means, the Company deems this right not applicable to those within the scope of this policy at this time.

Right to Object

Article 21 of the GDPR gives individuals the Right to Object to the processing of their personal data. This effectively allows individuals to ask you to stop processing their personal data. The Right to Object only applies in certain circumstances. Whether it applies depends on your purposes for processing and your lawful basis for processing.

You always have the Right to Object to any direct marketing we conduct, although we would only carry our direct marketing where we have sought your Consent through an 'opt-in' process and provided you with a straightforward means of withdrawing your Consent at any time.

Any objections for processing which we do on the basis of Legitimate Interests or Public Task must be specific to the situation. We would need to demonstrate a compelling reason why our interests outweigh your interests, rights and freedoms if we were not to uphold your objection.

Any requests made in this regard will be treated in line with our Standard Procedures for Handling Requests.

Further details and examples of the types of information we process are available in our Privacy Notice.

Responsibilities

Due to our size, we have determined that we are not required to have a Data Protection Officer (DPO), as we do not process special categories of data on a large scale. Nonetheless, to ensure that every individual’s data rights are respected and that there is the highest levels of data security and protection in our organisation, the Company has designated an Information Governance (IG) Lead role. The IG Lead will report to the highest management level of the organisation. The Company will support the IG Lead with the necessary resources to carry out their tasks and ensure that they can maintain expertise.

Our Information Governance Lead

Our designated IG Lead is Tim Whalley. The key responsibilities of the role are:

  • to ensure that the rights of individuals in terms of their personal data are upheld in all instances and that data collection, sharing and storage is in line with the Caldicott Principles;
  • to define our Data Protection Policy and procedures and all related policies, procedures and processes and to ensure that sufficient resources are provided to support the policy requirements;
  • to complete the Data Security and Protection Toolkit (DSP Toolkit) annually and to maintain compliance with the DSP Toolkit;
  • oversee changes to systems and processes;
  • monitor compliance with the GDPR;
  • complete a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA);
  • to represent all aspects of information and data protection and security to senior management and drive engagement in data protection at the highest levels of the organisation;
  • to liaise, if required, with the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO).

Documentation of Processing Activities

Caldicott Principle No. 1 states:

Every proposed use or transfer of personal confidential data within or from an organisation should be clearly defined, scrutinised and documented, with continuing uses regularly reviewed, by an appropriate guardian.

Information Guidance Documents

Our Privacy Notice provides further details about exactly what information we may collect about you; who may have access to it; who we may share it with and under what legal bases we process this information by. In addition, the Company has plain-language guides for staff and residents to help them understand how we handle their personal data. These documents are available in hardcopy at the premises or by request and on the Company website.

Information Asset Register (IAR)

The Information Asset Register will record details of all personal information that the Company processes including:

  • A description of the document or file concerned;
  • how it is categorised;
  • where it is stored;
  • who is responsible for the management of the record;
  • whether it contains sensitive information;
  • details of any transfers that the record is subject to;
  • our lawful basis for processing;
  • how long the record is retained;
  • details of any breach the record has been subject to;
  • any security measures that have been applied;
  • any risk assessments that have taken place;
  • when the last review was conducted

We have chosen to record all types of asset that contain personal information, however, in certain instances when a number of separate documents or files make up one composite document or file and all the individual elements can be treated in the same way, then it is the composite document or file that is recorded on the register.

Record of Processing Activities (ROPA)

In addition, a Record of Processing Activities will record details of all sensitive personal information, generally referred to as either Special Category Data or Criminal Records Data. Both of these types of data have additional controls in place to protect them and these arrangements will be recorded on the ROPA.

Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)

A Data Protection Impact Assessment will be conducted ahead of any new system or process that is likely to result in a high risk to individuals. The assessment will both the likelihood and severity of any impact on an individual and ensure that appropriate measures are put in place to minimise the risk.

Third-party Processor Contracts

Wherever we, as a Data Controller use a third-party to process personal information a written contract will be in place to ensure that the responsibilities of both parties are understood

Secure Disposal Records

A record will be made of all documents or electronic files that have reached the end of their retention period and our due for secure disposal. This record will be countersigned by the IG Lead prior to disposal as per the correct procedure.

Information Security Audit

An audit will be completed at least annually to ensure that information is secured properly and that access is restricted to those who have a legal requirement to use the information. In addition, the Data Security and Protection Toolkit (DSP Toolkit) will be renewed each year as part of our obligations.

Data Security Policy

Article 5(1)(f) of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) concerns the ‘integrity and confidentiality’ of personal data. It says that personal data shall be:

Processed in a manner that ensures appropriate security of the personal data, including protection against unauthorised or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction or damage, using appropriate technical or organisational measures

Our Data Security Policy sets out procedures that ensure:

  • the safekeeping of all records from their creation to disposal;
  • the safe sharing of information externally when there is a legal basis to do so;
  • personal information is protected and that it is not disclosed inappropriately, either by accident or design, whilst in use or when it is being transferred;
  • all records are retained and destroyed in-line with the Retention & Disposal Procedures.
  • preserve the confidentiality and accuracy of all electronic assets;
  • protect those assets against unauthorised disclosure.

Access to Records

Caldicott Principle No. 4 states:

Only those individuals who need access to personal confidential data should have access to it, and they should only have access to the data items that they need to see. This may mean introducing access controls or splitting data flows where one data flow is used for several purposes.

Physical access to records shall only be granted on a strict ‘need to know’ basis. Access to electronic records shall be granted using the principle that every program and every user of the system should operate using the least set of privileges necessary to complete their job.

During their induction each staff member who requires access to confidential information for their job role will be trained on the safe handling of all information and will be taught the procedures which govern how data is used, stored, shared and organised in our organisation.

Staff must retain personal and confidential information and data securely in locked storage when not in use, and keys should not be left in the barrels of filing cabinets and doors.

Methods of Sharing Confidential Information

Caldicott Principle No. 7 states:

Health and social care professionals should have the confidence to share information in the best interests of their patients within the framework set out by these principles. They should be supported by the policies of their employers, regulators and professional bodies.

We ensure that there are secure points for the receipt of personal information transferred to us and we have applied the following measures to safeguard personal information during receipt and transfer/transit.

Written & Verbal Communication

Staff members have been provided with training in relation to data protection. They know that they must take appropriate precautions not to reveal confidential information e.g. to avoid being overheard when making a phone call or not to have confidential conversations in public places or open offices. The Employee Handbook and training informs them that breach of this procedure may be a disciplinary or legal offense (see our Confidentiality Code of Conduct).

Postal Services and Couriers

We will ensure that all confidential information that we transfer by post or courier is done so as securely as is practicable. All records transferred in this manner are addressed to a named individual and marked “Private and Confidential”. All records which are posted will be done through signed-for delivery so that it is guaranteed that the correct person receives the record.

Faxes

The fax machine is located in the Admin Office. To ensure that confidential information transferred by fax is done so as securely as is practicable, we ensure that:

  • the fax number is always double checked, and frequently used numbers are stored in the fax machine to reduce the risk of typing errors;
  • a fax cover sheet is used and marked "Private and Confidential";
  • faxes are only sent to a named person rather than a team;
  • the recipient is informed that a fax will be sent, and asked to confirm receipt;
  • faxes are not sent outside a recipient organisation's working hours where there is no-one present to receive;
  • we regularly check the date and time, especially following power outages, or change of British summer time;
  • logs are retained in line with our retention schedule;

For improved data security, the use of faxes will be replaced by encrypted email (see Email).

Email

The Company does not currently have access to secure email, until such time, the procedure is to never send or receive sensitive personal information via email.

It is the intention of the Company to apply for access to the NHSMail system which will allow secure transfer of information via this medium. The policy will be updated once this is in place

Before sending any email containing confidential information:

  • consider whether this is the most appropriate form of distribution;
  • check the content for accuracy and to ensure it is up-to-date and relevant;
  • check recipient address(es) for accuracy to ensure that the email is not sent to the wrong person;
  • consider whether any recipients on the distribution list may not expect their email addresses to be shared with others;
  • consider encrypting any attachments where resources of both sender and receiver allow;
  • ensure that you use the email signature that has been provided and approved by the Company, including the Company’s legal disclaimer for emails.

Whilst the Company takes strict measures to prevent incoming ‘spam’ emails, it is inevitable that some will find their way to inboxes. Unsolicited emails from unknown sources should be treated with suspicion. In such emails, never click any links nor open attachments. If you have an unusually high number of such emails, please contact the IG Lead.

Just as the use of the postal address of the Company should not be used for personal deliveries, so a Company email address should not be used to receive personal emails. However, if an email of a non-work-related nature is received, on no account should the email be forwarded to others. The use of a company email address to register for any personal online account or nonbusiness-related services is also forbidden.

Card Payments (PCI DSS Compliance)

Compliance with Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS) is mandatory for any company that stores, processes, or transmits payment cardholder data. The Company does not store or transmit payment card data but does process card payments which are subsequently handled by an external service provider who is certified as being PCI DSS compliant.

Arrangements

A Cardholder Data Environment (CDE) has been defined. The storage, operation and maintenance of any equipment used to process card payments is restricted to this area only. The processing of card payments is only done by authorised employees who have had specific instruction in this regard. A register of these employees is maintained.

Responsibilities

  • The Information Governance (IG) Lead has responsibility for compliance with PCI DSS;
  • The Company will provide the necessary training and resources to staff with responsibilities for taking card payments;
  • Authorised users should be aware of all devices and technologies located in the CDE and should not add or remove anything without the explicit consent of the IG Lead;
  • The policy will be reviewed annually by the IG Lead and updated where necessary to reflect changes to business objectives, risk environment, systems, authorised personnel or the business’s merchant level;
  • The IG Lead will also complete the mandatory annual self-assessment questionnaire (SAQ), issued by the PCI Security Standards Council, to demonstrate compliance.

Processing Payments

  1. Staff who process card payments must fully comply with the procedures for managing cardholder data;
  2. The card payment device or any other technologies are not to be used for any purpose other than official business;
  3. No sensitive data relating to card payments is to be stored on computers. If it is necessary to physically record the cardholder’s name, Primary Account Number(PAN), the expiry date of the card or service code, this must be done so on paper and must be kept locked away and shredded at the first convenient opportunity;
  4. The Card Verification Number should not be physically recorded under any circumstances;
  5. All merchant copies of receipts must have the PAN masked;
  6. All sensitive data is shredded either directly or using the locked confidential waste bins;
  7. Privately-owned computers and other equipment (including mobile telephones, laptop computers and tablets) must not be used for the processing, storage or transmission of any cardholder data associated with any aspect of the business;
  8. There must be no distribution of any media used to record cardholder data outside of the CDE;
  9. Cameras, mobile telephones (incorporating cameras) and visual, and audio recording devices must not be used in the CDE.

Permissions and Systems Maintenance

The IG Lead must be informed and grant permission in any of the following instances:

  • any requests for technical support for components within the CDE;
  • the addition or removal of any system or electronic device within the CDE;
  • the processing or storage of payment card data on any computer or transmission via any network.

Incident Response

The IG Lead must be informed immediately of any suspected breach in operating procedures or following a suspected or actual malicious action involving any devices within the CDE.

Retention Schedule & Disposal Procedures

All staff must follow the retention schedule for records in our organisation. At the end of their lifespan, the records will go through an appraisal process. This process will determine if there is a continuing legal basis for keeping the record. The IG Lead will have final responsibility for determining whether the record will be destroyed or retained. They will maintain a record of all retention or disposal decisions.

The Company will adhere to the retention timelines determined by the Information Governance Alliance (IGA) in Appendix 3 of Records Management Code of Practise for Health and Social Care 2016.

The destruction of records will comply with the British Security Industry Association EN 15713:2009 standard. the Company uses a third-party contractor to securely dispose of records, and has a contract which specifies that they comply with this standard. Disposal certificates will be kept in line with your retention schedule.

Data Security Breach Procedures

All staff are trained to recognise a potential data breach and understand the procedures to follow and how to escalate a security incident to the correct person in order to determine if a breach has taken place;

In the instance that it appears that a data security breach has taken place:

  • The staff member who notices the breach, or potential breach, will complete a Data Security Breach Incident Report Form;
  • This form will be completed and handed to the IG Lead or, if they are not available, to a member of senior management;
  • The IG Lead will complete the rest of the Incident Report Form and conduct a thorough investigation into the breach;
  • In the instance that the breach is a personal data breach and it is likely that there will be a risk to the rights and freedoms of an individual then the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) will be informed as soon as possible, but at least within 72 hours via their website: https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/report-a-breach/;
  • As part of our report we will provide the ICO with the following details:
    • The nature of the personal data breach (i.e. confidentiality, integrity, availability);
    • The approximate number of individuals concerned and the category of individual (e.g. employees, mailing lists, residents);
    • The categories and approximate number of personal data records concerned;
    • The name and details of our IG Lead;
    • The likely consequences of the breach;
    • A description of the measures taken, or which we will take, to mitigate any possible adverse effects.
      • The IG Lead will inform any individual that their personal data has been breached if it is likely that there is a high risk to their rights and freedoms. We will inform them directly and without any undue delay;
      • A data security breach will be marked on the IAR and a prompt an audit of all processes will take place in order to correct any procedure which led to the breach.

Network Security Policy

This document sets out the Company's policy for the protection of the confidentiality, integrity and availability of the Company's Data Network. It establishes the security responsibilities for network security and provides reference to documentation relevant to this policy. To do this the Company will:

  • ensure the availability of critical services;
  • ensure that access to the Company network is restricted to Registered Users;
  • protect the network from unauthorised or accidental modification;

This policy applies to anyone who has access to the Company network either from within the Local Area Network (LAN) or via remote access. It applies to any devices that connect to the network to access data locally or in order to create an external connection (eg. the internet). This policy applies to processes involving:

  • the storage, sharing and transmission of non-clinical data and images;
  • the storage, sharing and transmission of clinical data and images;
  • printing or scanning non-clinical or clinical data or images;
  • the provision of Internet systems for receiving, sending and storing non-clinical or clinical data or images.

The policy is intended for anyone using such devices whether as an employee, contractor, visitor, volunteer or service user.

The NHS Digital website provides extensive guidance on network security.

Physical Security

Critical assets will be protected against fire by suitable fire prevention procedures and an automatic fire detection system; against power failure or damage by power surge by uninterruptible power supplies (UPS) and a standby generator, and against theft or unauthorised entry by security locks and restricted access rules.

When not in use all display screens will be locked and a clear screen policy will be followed. There is guidance on clear screen policies on the NHS Digital website.

The Information Governance (IG) Lead is responsible for:

  • safeguarding keys and ensuring that code locks are changed periodically;
  • for the integrity of the security measures
  • authorising all visitors to secure network areas and for making visitors aware of network security requirements;
  • ensuring that all staff are made aware of procedures for visitors and contractors and that visitors are escorted and contractors supervised when necessary;

Network Access Control

In order to gain access to the network, Registered Users will be authenticated by a secure log-on credentials, designed to minimise the opportunity for unauthorised access. Remote access to the network is strictly limited to essential users and administrators only and is also password protected.

The Company encourages password management procedures following guidance on the NHS Digital website and the National Cyber Security Centre.

Where software and systems allow, the Company will ensure that each user is identified by a unique user ID so that users can be linked to and be accountable for their actions. A record is kept of all users given access to the Company Network and its system.

The use of group IDs is only permitted where they are suitable for the work carried out (such as in the case of departmental email accounts).

The IG Lead will ensure that:

  • all users of the network are provided with the necessary security guidance, awareness and where appropriate training to discharge their security responsibilities;
  • access rights to the network will be allocated on the requirements of the user's job, rather than on a status basis.
  • only Registered Users are able to access the network and are de-registered when access is no longer required;
  • user access rights will be immediately removed or reviewed for those users who have left the Company or if their role changes.
  • security privileges (i.e. "superuser" or network administrator rights) to the network will be allocated on the requirements of the user's job, rather than on a status basis.
  • good password management practices are followed;
  • other than through a formal contract with a company providing IT support, all third-party access to the network is only granted in exceptional circumstances in order to provide a beneficial service.
  • there is a regular review of all access rights, at least once a year. The review is designed to positively confirm all system users. Any lapsed or unwanted logons which are identified are disabled immediately and deleted unless positively reconfirmed.

The IG Lead must approve all connections to external networks and systems before they commence operation.

The external connection is a dedicated line providing point to point connectivity.

A suitable firewall is in place with policies to prevent unauthorised access and to protect corporate devices against malicious websites.

Electronic Communications - Acceptable Use Policy

Scope

This policy covers the use of all forms of electronic communication including email, fax or telephone calls, social media, SMS (text messaging) or any form of voice or video transmissions either live or recorded.

Etiquette

employees should consider all communications made from Company assets or using Company networks, including anything uploaded to the internet which makes reference to Company business, as Company property.

Electronic communication should not be used as a substitute for face to face or telephone conversations or meetings.

All electronic communications should be clear and unambiguous and used primarily to distribute/update information, confirm arrangements, confirm meetings etc.

It must not be used for purposes outside of the job role or for any personal reasons.

Email should be treated as if it were a hard copy letter and drafted and checked in the same way. The Company will value an email message the same as a hard copy letter.

All communications must adhere to the principles set out in the Company’s Data Protection Policy. Where confidential information is being sent, either directly or via attachments in email or hyperlinks, appropriate controls must be used to ensure that this information is not at risk of a breach.

The same standards of behaviour are expected in electronic communication as apply to verbal communication in the workplace. No email message or response to an email should be sent in haste, anger or hostility. Ideally a time for consideration should elapse between drafting and sending a reply. Email messages should be checked after drafting for clarity, accuracy of message and absence of words which could be abusive, emotive, etc. The language used must be consistent with conventional standards, decency and respect for others and good manners. Commenting on or about another person should be avoided but, if it is unavoidable, should only be based on and backed up by facts. On no account must any Company communication system or any social media platform be used for vindictive, harassing, discriminatory or abusive comment or criticism of anyone, whether this is to target the Company, another employee or any third-party. Furthermore, it may not be used for electronic stalking. Words in capital letters could be considered shouting. The use of exclamation marks, humour and sarcasm can be especially problematic in emails and should be used with caution. Please consider the ‘tone’ of your message in the same way you would speak to someone face to face.

employees using any public digital platform outside of work must ensure that they do not make any reference to the Home by name or in photographs, or to any employee, resident or any other person connected with the company other than in an entirely positive manner.

Disciplinary Action

Company websites and social media sites are the sole property of the Company and any unauthorised interference with or copying and variation of such websites is not only gross misconduct but also can generate an action for damages. A person receiving an email which they feel contravenes these rules should notify their line manager immediately. On no account should such concerns, which may become subject to an investigation, be discussed with other employees as this would be viewed as gossip and subject to the relevant policy and disciplinary action. Any person proved to have deliberately used email in a way that contravenes the policy will be deemed guilty of gross misconduct and will be subject to disciplinary procedures. Failure to comply with any aspect of this policy will be treated as a serious breach of the rules and will result in disciplinary action.

The Company will treat all financial and personal information as confidential and is committed to making the protection of confidential information an essential part of the business, understood by every employee and embedded into all systems and processes.

The Company respects the right to privacy and where information is stored and shared, to do so with full knowledge and consent of the individual concerned and in line with its own policies and legal obligations. The Company recognises the need to balance confidentiality with transparency, and of security with ease of access to records.

The Company is principally a Data Controller but may also act as the Data Processor in certain circumstances.

The Company records, stores and shares financial and personal information, including Sensitive Personal Information (such as employee health records) and Special Category Data (such as care plans and criminial records checks) using a variety of physical and digital media in order to fulfill a wide range of business functions. The Company is registered with the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) as the Data Controller.

The policies contained within The Information Governance Handbook set out the ways in which the Company safeguards confidential information within the business and should be understood by all members of staff, agency workers, volunteers, students and any relevant contractor and anyone who has access to the Company Data Network either from within the Local Area Network or via remote access. There is also the need for residents and their next of kin, visitors to our website or any customers to have an understanding of certain aspects of the way we operate.

The designated Information Governance (IG) Lead is responsible for overseeing day to day Information Governance issues; developing and maintaining policies, standards, procedures and guidance, completing risk assessments and introducing suitable controls, raising awareness of Information Governance within the Company and ensuring that there is ongoing compliance with the policy and its supporting standards and guidelines.

The Company Board is responsible for ensuring that sufficient resources are available to support the implementation of Information Governance procedures in order to ensure compliance with legal and professional requirements and the NHS Information Governance requirements.

All those within the scope of this document have a responsibility to safeguard confidential information at all times as specified in the Company’s Confidentiality Code of Conduct. To store data securely as per specified procedures or recognised standards and codes of practice and be aware of situations where disclosure may be required.

The Company will ensure that suitable training and resources are made available to staff in order for them to perform their duties. This includes induction training and Information Governance training. The suitability and effectiveness of the Information Governance Handbook will be reviewed annually and where necessary, changes and improvements will be actioned accordingly.

Breach of any of the policies in this handbook could lead to disciplinary action. Depending on the circumstances this could range from remedial training to dismissal.
